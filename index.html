Sheaf Semantics for Machine Learning

Machine learning is good at finding patterns in data. However, when machine learning is used to try and learn like humans, it often fails because humans understand the world through high order semantic abstractions.

As machines become increasingly intelligent, we are increasingly describing them as silicon incarnations of life learning about the world. But a critical distinction between machines and humans is the way in which we reason about our world. In contrast, machines blindly search for the strongest signals in a pile of data. Lacking a cushion of background knowledge or life experiences to understand the meaning of those signals, they cannot distinguish between meaningful and spurious indicators. A human, shown a collection of photographs of dogs running around dog parks and house cats running around people's homes, understands that in this particular context, the background of the photograph is not relevant...
